001

Offline Experiences

Everyday women around Europe face a various form of harassment that are normalized forms of sexism and misogyny. These forms of violence are so common to become normal and women complaining about are seen as overreacting, lacking of sense of humor, or considered a bit frigid or uptight and need to learn to take a compliment.

Perpetrators & different forms of sexual harassment

In most cases of sexual harassment faced by women, the perpetrator is an unknown person (68%), followed by somebody the respondent knows (35%) or somebody from the employment context such as a colleague, supervisor or a client (32%). In the survey, women were able to identify one or more perpetrator categories, based on their experiences, so the percentages of various perpetrator categories add up to over 100%. Victims of sexual harassment typically name perpetrators from more than one perpetrator category.

Emotional response

Emotional response of sexual harassment refer to women’s experiences that involved various feelings. In the immediate aftermath of the violence the majority of women exhibit symptoms of anger and annoyance as a result, followed by embarrassment, fear, shock, shame and aggressiveness.

002

Online Experiences

Online misogyny is a global gender rights tragedy. It is routine for many women to be harassed and verbally assaulted on the Internet. It happens on social media platforms such as Twitter, Instagram, Facebook and many other platforms affecting more or less gravely women’s mental as an actual real-life violence.

Cyberharassment in Europe

% of women experiencing cyberharassment since the age of 15.

The online world is not immune to many of the human rights abuses that women face offline. In fact anonymity facilitates the spread of harassment online. This kind of abuse includes direct or indirect threats of physical or sexual violence, discriminatory abuse targeting one or more aspects of a woman’s identity, targeted harassment, and privacy violations such as doxing or sharing sexual or intimate images of a woman without her consent.

Online Perpetrators

For the online survey the perpetrators are divided by level of intimacy with the victim. The closer ones are the partner and the people the victim know personally, followed by people known online and friends of friends or family. For every state the most wide category is the stranger people, who often take advantage of their anonymity to harass women.

Platform and policies

Hate speech is an unfortunate common occurrence on the Internet and in some cases it culminates in severe threats to individuals. Most women feel that social media companies need to do more. Social media platforms, especially for women and marginalized groups, should be a critical space for individuals to exercise their rights and opinions. Instead, online violence and abuse are a direct threat to the freedom of expression.
